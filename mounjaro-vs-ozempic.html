Key Differences

Mechanism of Action

Mounjaro is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, meaning it activates two different pathways. Ozempic is a GLP-1-only agonist. This may explain why Mounjaro shows slightly higher weight loss in studies.

Savings Difference

The biggest practical difference is the savings card limit. Mounjaro offers 4x more savings per month ($650 vs $150), which matters if your insurance has high copays or coinsurance.

Insurance Coverage

Check your insurance formulary. Some plans prefer one medication over the other. If both are covered equally, Mounjaro's higher savings card makes it the better financial choice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch from Ozempic to Mounjaro?

Yes, but consult your doctor first. The switch requires a new prescription and insurance approval. Many patients successfully transition between the two medications.

Do both savings cards require insurance?

Yes. Both Mounjaro and Ozempic copay cards require commercial (private) insurance. Medicare and Medicaid are not eligible. However, both manufacturers offer Patient Assistance Programs for uninsured patients who meet income requirements.

Which is more effective for weight loss?

Clinical trials show Mounjaro leads to slightly more weight loss (15-22% vs 12-15% for Ozempic). However, individual results vary. Neither is FDA-approved for weight loss alone — Wegovy (semaglutide) is approved for weight management.

How long does enrollment take?

Both programs allow instant enrollment online. You can complete the application in 5-10 minutes and receive a digital savings card immediately. Some pharmacies can also enroll you at the counter.
