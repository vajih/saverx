About Respiratory Savings Programs

Respiratory medications — from daily inhalers to biologic add-ons — can cost $300–$800/month without coverage. Manufacturer savings programs are widely available for inhalers from AstraZeneca, GSK, and Boehringer Ingelheim.

Combination inhalers (ICS/LABA/LAMA): Symbicort, Breo Ellipta, Trelegy, and Breztri are the most common long-term controller inhalers for asthma and COPD. All have manufacturer savings programs capping costs at $10–$35/month for commercially insured patients.

Respiratory biologics (Fasenra): Biologic add-on therapies for severe eosinophilic asthma can cost $3,000–$4,000/month at retail, but manufacturer programs often bring costs to $0/month for commercially insured patients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Symbicort have a savings card?

Yes. AstraZeneca offers a Symbicort savings card for eligible commercially insured patients. Depending on the program, costs may be capped at $10–$35/month. Uninsured patients may qualify for the AstraZeneca Patient Assistance Program for Symbicort.

How much does Breo Ellipta cost with insurance?

GSK offers a Breo Ellipta savings card for eligible commercially insured patients, often capping costs at $10–$30/month. Without insurance, Breo Ellipta retails around $400–$500/month. Uninsured patients may qualify for the GSK Patient Assistance Program.

Is there a Fasenra savings program?

Yes. AstraZeneca's Fasenra One savings program offers eligible commercially insured patients Fasenra for as little as $0/month. The patient assistance program provides free medication to qualifying uninsured patients.

What is the cheapest asthma inhaler alternative?

Some asthma medications have generic versions (e.g., generic fluticasone/salmeterol). For branded inhalers like Symbicort, Breo Ellipta, and Trelegy, manufacturer savings cards are the most effective cost reduction for commercially insured patients. GoodRx coupons can also reduce costs significantly.
