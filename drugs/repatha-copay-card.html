Repatha® Copay Card & Savings Guide (2026)

What is Repatha?

Repatha® (evolocumab) is a prescription biologic medication manufactured by Amgen. It belongs to a class of drugs called PCSK9 inhibitors — injectable monoclonal antibodies that dramatically lower LDL ("bad") cholesterol.

Repatha works by blocking the PCSK9 protein, which normally prevents the liver from removing LDL cholesterol from the blood. By inhibiting PCSK9, Repatha allows the liver to clear far more LDL — often reducing levels by 60% or more on top of statin therapy.

Who uses Repatha?

  • Adults with high LDL not controlled by statins alone — As an add-on to diet and statin therapy when LDL remains too high.
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH) patients — Both heterozygous (HeFH) and homozygous (HoFH) forms. The 420mg monthly dose is specifically FDA-approved for HoFH.
  • Adults with established cardiovascular disease (CVD) — Repatha is approved to reduce the risk of heart attack, stroke, and coronary revascularization in adults who have had a prior cardiovascular event.
  • Statin-intolerant patients — If you cannot tolerate statins due to muscle pain or other side effects and your LDL remains dangerously high, Repatha may be an option.

You administer Repatha yourself at home via a subcutaneous injection using a prefilled SureClick® autoinjector or syringe. The most common dose is 140 mg every 2 weeks or 420 mg once monthly (given as three consecutive 140 mg injections).

What Does Repatha Cost Without Insurance?

Repatha is expensive. Without any savings program, the out-of-pocket cost is significant:

Monthly (2 autoinjectors, 140 mg/mL each) $620 – $700
Annual (no savings program) $7,400 – $8,400
With REPATHA SUPPORT™ copay card As low as $5/month
With Patient Assistance Program (uninsured) Free (income-based)

Why is Repatha so expensive?

  • It's a biologic: Unlike small-molecule pills, Repatha is a laboratory-engineered monoclonal antibody. Manufacturing biologics is complex, tightly regulated, and significantly more costly than making traditional drugs.
  • No generic equivalent: There is a biosimilar (Repatha SureClick has limited competition), but access varies by plan and formulary. Most patients still pay for the brand.
  • Specialty tier placement: Insurance plans typically place Repatha on Tier 4 or 5 (specialty), meaning your coinsurance or copay can be hundreds of dollars per month without assistance.
  • Prior authorization burden: PA is almost universally required, which can delay access and sometimes lead patients to pay out-of-pocket while waiting for approval.

The good news: Most commercially insured patients who qualify can reduce their monthly cost to just $5 using the official Amgen copay program. If you have no insurance, the Patient Assistance Program may cover the full cost. Keep reading for exact steps.

The REPATHA SUPPORT™ Copay Card

Amgen offers the REPATHA SUPPORT™ Enrollment Program, which includes a copay card that dramatically reduces your monthly cost if you have commercial (private) health insurance.

What you can save

  • Pay as little as $5 per month for your Repatha prescription.
  • The program covers up to $3,600 per year in out-of-pocket costs (approximately $300/month in maximum benefit).
  • There is no limit on the number of times you can use it within the benefit year, up to the annual maximum.

Who qualifies?

  • You must have commercial health insurance (employer-sponsored, individual marketplace, or similar private plan).
  • Your insurance plan must cover Repatha — the copay card bridges your out-of-pocket cost, but is not a substitute for insurance coverage.
  • You must be a U.S. resident and meet the program's terms and conditions.
  • You must not be enrolled in Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, TRICARE, or any other government-funded health program.

Important restriction: Federal law prohibits the use of manufacturer copay assistance cards with Medicare, Medicaid, or other government programs. Using the card while enrolled in a government plan is a federal healthcare violation. See the Medicare and uninsured options section below for legal alternatives.

Key program details

  • The savings card must be renewed annually. Your enrollment does not automatically carry over to the next benefit year.
  • The copay card applies to your out-of-pocket cost (copay, coinsurance, or deductible) as billed by the pharmacy — it does not affect what your insurance pays.
  • Not valid if Repatha is not covered by your insurance plan. If your plan does not cover Repatha, you would need to pay the full retail price and the card cannot be applied to that.
  • Available at specialty pharmacies only (Repatha is a specialty drug — it cannot be filled at most retail chain pharmacies).

Step-by-Step: How to Get the Repatha Copay Card

Follow these steps in order. Skipping steps — especially prior authorization — is the most common reason patients pay full price.

  1. Confirm you have commercial insurance — not Medicare or Medicaid

    Check your insurance card. If it says Medicare, Medicaid, or any state/federal program, skip to the Medicare & Uninsured Options section below. The copay card only works with private commercial insurance.

  2. Get your Repatha prescription and prior authorization started

    Ask your cardiologist or primary care doctor to send a Repatha prescription to your specialty pharmacy and to submit a prior authorization (PA) to your insurance. The PA requires documentation that statins were tried and either failed to adequately lower your LDL, or that you could not tolerate them. Without the PA, your insurance will likely deny coverage. This process typically takes 1–3 weeks.

  3. Enroll in the REPATHA SUPPORT™ program

    Visit repatha.com/patient/savings-and-support or call 1-844-REPATHA (1-844-737-2842). Have your insurance card handy. Complete the enrollment form — you will need your name, date of birth, insurance information, and prescribing doctor's name. You can also have your doctor's office enroll you on your behalf.

  4. Download, print, or save your savings card

    After enrollment is confirmed, Amgen will provide your savings card digitally (email or download) or by mail. You can also show the card directly on your phone at most specialty pharmacies. Save the card number and expiration date somewhere accessible.

  5. Contact your specialty pharmacy before your first fill

    Repatha is typically dispensed through specialty pharmacies such as Accredo, CVS Specialty, or Walgreens Specialty Pharmacy. Call the pharmacy directly to (a) confirm they accept the REPATHA SUPPORT™ copay card and (b) confirm your PA has been approved by insurance before they ship. This prevents getting surprised by a $600+ bill at your door.

  6. Provide the card at each fill and confirm your $5 charge

    Every time you fill your prescription, provide your copay card number. Your charge should be $5 or as close to $0 as your card benefit allows. Ask the pharmacist to confirm the card was applied before completing the transaction. If the total seems wrong, do not pay — ask them to rerun the card or call Amgen support directly.

  7. Renew your card annually before it expires

    Set a calendar reminder one month before your card expiration. Renewal requires re-confirming eligibility but is usually fast. If you miss renewal and fill without a valid card, you will be charged the full insurance out-of-pocket cost.

Medicare, Medicaid & Uninsured: Your Options

If the REPATHA SUPPORT™ copay card is not available to you, there are still pathways to reduce or eliminate your cost.

Medicare Part D patients

  • Medicare Extra Help (Low Income Subsidy / LIS): If your income and resources are below certain limits, this federal program can significantly reduce your Part D premiums, deductibles, and copays — including for specialty drugs like Repatha. Apply through Social Security (ssa.gov) or call 1-800-772-1213.
  • Medicare Savings Programs (MSPs): State-run programs that help with Medicare costs for low-income beneficiaries. Your State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP) counselor can help you identify what you qualify for. Find your local SHIP at shiphelp.org.
  • Review your Part D formulary annually: Some Medicare Part D plans cover PCSK9 inhibitors more favorably than others. During open enrollment (October 15 – December 7), compare plans at medicare.gov/plan-compare and look for a plan with lower Repatha cost-sharing.
  • Ask about the Medicare Inflation Reduction Act cap: Starting in 2025, Medicare Part D out-of-pocket drug costs are capped at $2,000 per year. If Repatha is your main expense, you may reach this cap earlier in the year than you think.

Uninsured or underinsured patients

REPATHA SUPPORT™ Patient Assistance Program (PAP): Amgen offers free Repatha to qualifying uninsured or underinsured patients through their patient assistance program. Income eligibility typically extends to patients earning up to 600% of the federal poverty level (FPL). To apply, visit amgen.com/patients/patient-assistance or call 1-844-737-2842. Your doctor will need to participate in the application.

  • NeedyMeds.org: A free, independent database of drug manufacturer assistance programs. Search "evolocumab" or "Repatha" at needymeds.org.
  • RxAssist.org: Maintained by Volunteers in Health Care, this database lists manufacturer patient assistance programs and eligibility criteria. Visit rxassist.org.
  • State Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs (SPAPs): Some states run their own drug assistance programs. Your local Area Agency on Aging or state social services office can tell you if one is available in your state.
  • Marketplace insurance with subsidies: If you are uninsured and your income qualifies, you may be eligible for significant premium subsidies on ACA marketplace plans at healthcare.gov. A plan with Repatha coverage plus the copay card can dramatically cut your cost.

Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

These are the mistakes that most often cause patients to pay far more than they should — or to give up on treatment entirely.

  • Trying to fill Repatha at a retail pharmacy.
    Repatha is a specialty drug. Standard retail pharmacies (CVS, Walgreens, Rite Aid) almost never carry it. You must fill through a specialty pharmacy — and confirm they accept your copay card before you fill. Calling ahead is not optional.
  • Filling before the prior authorization is approved.
    If you fill Repatha before your insurance approves the PA, you will almost certainly be charged the full cash price. Wait for written or verbal PA approval from your pharmacy or insurer before filling.
  • Using the copay card with Medicare or Medicaid.
    This is a federal compliance issue — it cannot be used, period. If you are on Medicare, see the Medicare options above. There are legitimate alternatives.
  • Letting the enrollment card expire without renewing.
    Annual renewal is required. Many patients discover mid-year that their card expired months ago and they have been overpaying. Set a reminder at least 30 days before expiration.
  • Assuming the copay card works if Repatha is not covered by your plan.
    The copay card reduces your out-of-pocket portion of a covered drug. If Repatha is not on your formulary or is specifically excluded, the copay card cannot be applied. Your first step is always to confirm coverage.
  • Not appealing after a insurance denial.
    PA denials are common on first submission. You have the right to appeal, and appeals often succeed — especially with a letter of medical necessity from your cardiologist. Do not accept a denial as final before pursuing at least one appeal.

Pro Tips to Maximize Your Savings

  • Ask your cardiologist for a free starter sample.
    While your PA is being processed, ask your doctor if they have Repatha starter samples. Amgen provides starter sample kits to prescribing physicians. This can keep you on therapy for free during the 1–4 week PA window.
  • Enroll in REPATHA SUPPORT™ before your first fill — not after.
    Enrollment is instant online. Do it the same day your doctor sends the prescription, so the card is ready when the PA approves. There's no reason to wait.
  • Use Amgen's hub services for PA support.
    REPATHA SUPPORT™ includes a team that can help you navigate prior authorization and insurance appeals. Call 1-844-737-2842 and ask for PA assistance. This is a free service that can save enormous time.
  • Compare specialty pharmacies for convenience and reliability.
    Accredo, CVS Specialty, and Walgreens Specialty all dispense Repatha and accept the copay card. Some ship directly to your home with cold packing. Ask your doctor which specialty pharmacy is in-network for your plan to avoid extra fees.
  • If your plan changes at open enrollment, re-check your PA and card.
    Switching insurance plans can invalidate your prior authorization and your copay card enrollment simultaneously. Do it right: before switching plans, confirm your new plan covers Repatha, that a PA submission is ready, and re-enroll in the savings program under your new insurance.
  • Track your annual benefit usage.
    The copay card has a $3,600/year maximum. If you are approaching the cap (unlikely at $5/fill, but possible if your plan's cost-sharing is high), contact Amgen to explore additional support options before the benefit runs out.

Repatha Savings: Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use the Repatha copay card with Medicare?

No. The REPATHA SUPPORT™ copay card cannot be used by anyone enrolled in Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, TRICARE, or any other federal or state government health program. This is not a policy choice by Amgen — it is a requirement under federal anti-kickback law.

If you are on Medicare, your options include Medicare Part D Extra Help (Low Income Subsidy), Medicare Savings Programs, annual plan comparisons at medicare.gov, and the $2,000 annual out-of-pocket cap under the Inflation Reduction Act starting in 2025.

How much can I save with the Repatha copay card?

Eligible commercially insured patients may pay as little as $5 per month. The program covers up to $3,600 per year in out-of-pocket costs — which works out to up to $300/month in benefit. Your actual savings depend on your plan's cost-sharing for Repatha and whether the copay card fully covers your portion.

For most patients with commercial insurance that covers Repatha, the $5/month rate is achievable.

What if I have no insurance and cannot afford Repatha?

Amgen offers the REPATHA SUPPORT™ Patient Assistance Program for qualifying uninsured or underinsured patients. Eligible patients may receive Repatha at no cost. Income limits apply and are reviewed based on the federal poverty level.

You can apply at amgen.com/patients/patient-assistance or by calling 1-844-737-2842. Your doctor will need to sign off on the application. Additional resources include NeedyMeds.org and RxAssist.org.

Does Repatha require prior authorization from my insurance?

Yes — nearly universally. Most insurance plans require prior authorization before covering Repatha. The PA process typically requires your doctor to document that:

  • You have been diagnosed with hyperlipidemia, familial hypercholesterolemia, or established cardiovascular disease.
  • You have tried and failed to achieve adequate LDL reduction on maximally tolerated statin therapy (or cannot tolerate statins).

The PA review process usually takes 1–3 weeks. If denied, you have the right to appeal. Amgen's REPATHA SUPPORT™ team can assist with PA support — call 1-844-737-2842.

Where do I fill my Repatha prescription?

Repatha is a specialty medication and is dispensed through specialty pharmacies only — not typical retail or grocery pharmacies. Common options include:

  • Accredo (Express Scripts' specialty pharmacy) — 1-800-803-2523
  • CVS Specialty Pharmacy — 1-800-237-2767
  • Walgreens Specialty Pharmacy — 1-866-973-7503
  • Your insurer's preferred in-network specialty pharmacy (check your plan documents for the lowest cost option)

Most specialty pharmacies ship Repatha directly to your home with proper cold packing (Repatha must be refrigerated). Always confirm copay card acceptance with the pharmacy before first fill.

How often do I need to renew the Repatha savings card?

The REPATHA SUPPORT™ copay card must be renewed annually. Your enrollment does not automatically carry over from one benefit year to the next. You will typically receive a renewal reminder from Amgen, but it is wise to set your own calendar reminder 30 days before expiration.

Renewal is usually straightforward — you will need to re-confirm your insurance eligibility and agree to updated program terms. Call 1-844-737-2842 or renew online at repatha.com/patient/savings-and-support.

Can I use the Repatha copay card if my insurance requires step therapy?

Step therapy requirements are a separate matter from the copay card. If your plan requires you to first try (and fail on) a statin or another cholesterol drug before approving Repatha, you would need to complete that process — or your doctor would need to document a compelling clinical exception — before Repatha would be covered.

The copay card applies only once your insurance covers Repatha. If coverage is denied due to step therapy, work with your doctor to either fulfill the step therapy requirement or request a step therapy exception based on your medical history.
